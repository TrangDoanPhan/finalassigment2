Why food preservation is important

Preservation methods help inhibit bacterial growth meaning the food will be safe and satisfying in the future. Food preservation help minimise pathogenic bacteria and will stop its growth. Bacteria only needs warmth and moisture, so knowing how to prevent the minimization of bacteria will help to keep food at its best quality. Your food will last longer which will also help you to save money.
In the beginning, it can be hard to start practicing this into a daily habit. Therefore, we have come up with easy beginner tips that you can put into your daily routine. These tips are easy to get used to, to remember and to do.

Make a Smoothie



Fruit, vegetables, and herbs that you might think of discarding, may be used in a nutritious mix. This may include: overripe bananas, wilted herbs, broccoli or kale stems.

Vacuum Packing

Vacuum packing deprives bacteria of oxygen by creating an airtight atmosphere. You have to remember to prepare your food hygienically, put it in a suitable vacuum bag and when it's done you can store it in the freezer. (This does require a food vacuum machine)

Food Labelling

It's important to understand the difference between “use by” and “best before” dates. The “use by” date tells you when the food is no longer safe to eat. The “best before” dates are about quality, the food is still safe to eat after.

Shopping List


Write a shopping list and pre-decide your weekly dinner meals. If you plan your meals it will be easier for you when you go to the grocery store. You will have an intention and prevent overbuying.

Drying Food


Drying is a good way of preserving food like fruits, vegetables, herbs and meat. Most types of foods can be dried by using an oven, with a dehydrator or in the sun. Drying is also a way for you to save money, not wasting food by worrying so much about the expiration date. Dry food can be healthier, especially fruit that has a greater concentration of fiber and antioxidants than fresh fruits.

System in your refrigerator


It may be easier to keep a track of all your items if you have a system. Put the older items that expire soon in the front and the ones that expire in a while at the back. This will help you see the items you should use first when you eiter open your refrigerator, freezer or pantry.
